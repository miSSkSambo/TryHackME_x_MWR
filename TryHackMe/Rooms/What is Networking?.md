## 🎯 What This Room Is About

This room introduces the **basics of networking**, explaining how devices connect and communicate over networks and the Internet.

You learn:
- 🌍 What a network and the Internet are  
- 🆔 How devices are identified (IP & MAC addresses)  
- 📡 How communication works using protocols like ICMP  
- 🧪 Basic practical skills like **pinging devices** and **MAC spoofing**

👉 This forms the foundation for cybersecurity, since all attacks and defenses happen over networks.

## 📘 Task 3: Identifying Devices on a Network

**Q:** What does the term "IP" stand for?  
**A:** Internet Protocol  

**Q:** What is each section of an IPv4 address called?  
**A:** Octet  

**Q:** How many sections (octets) does an IPv4 address contain?  
**A:** 4  

**Q:** What does the term "MAC" stand for?  
**A:** Media Access Control  

**Q:** After spoofing your MAC address in the lab, what flag is obtained?  
**A:** THM{YOU_GOT_ON_TRYHACKME}  

---

## 📘 Task 4: Ping (ICMP)

**Q:** What protocol does the ping command use to communicate between devices?  
**A:** ICMP  

**Q:** What is the correct syntax to ping the IP address 10.10.10.10?  
**A:** ping 10.10.10.10  

**Q:** What flag is returned when you successfully ping 8.8.8.8?  
**A:** THM{I_PINGED_THE_SERVER}  

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways

✨ From this room you learn:
- Networks = devices connected together  
- The Internet = a network of networks  
- Devices are identified using:
  - 🌐 IP Address (logical, changeable)  
  - 🔌 MAC Address (physical, unique but spoofable)  
- Ping helps test connectivity using ICMP  
- Basic attacks like **MAC spoofing** can bypass weak security
